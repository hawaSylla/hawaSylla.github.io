Getting started... My first Hackathon !

4 minutes
February 3, 2021

Prejudmania is a game that I had worked on at the 2020 GSU Hackathon that my school had hosted. I was inspired not only by my personal experience but the short amount of time I had to complete it.

This being my first Hackathon, I wasn’t really sure what to expect. Though I was nervous at first, soon after the event, I wondered why I was even nervous at all ! In fact, I only wanted to participate in another one.

I am pretty proud of the winning result I got, though it did not come easy…

With the Hackathon being purely online and losing communication with my team several times, there were many obstacles I had to overcome, and if I wanted to submit something, I had to be quick on my feet. I revisited an idea I had come up with on the first day, and got to work on that . That idea? A game called prejudmania. I tried to do something quick since at the time of starting the Unity project, I had 1.5 days left in the Hackathon ! (Talk about close.)

So anyways, I took a look at the sketches that I came up with, and then digitally illustrated those with my iPad and Procreate. After coming up with some “enemy” sprites, and our main character, Ida, I went ahead and imported them into Unity.

Then after spending hours trying to make a “Galaga” style shooter… I realized I was reaching above my means.

Keep in mind: the important thing about being a Developer is knowing when to take a break , and move on. Especially when you technically only have 18 hours left to submit a game. So that is what I did. I moved on.

At this point, I had even less time to finish. But, at this point, one cannot simply give up. I lost way too much sleep and spent way too much time on my screen to do that. So I decided to revisit simplicity.

An Endless runner. Which is what I came up with. It was easy to come up with the player controller, a really, REALLY simple UI, and I ended up using one of the enemy sprite iterations.

Then, 3 coffee’s, 2 Street Fighter soundtracks, and several hours later, I finally had my final product, some screenshots, and a screen recording of gameplay.

“But Hawa, What about your GitHub? what about a build file ?”

MY FRIEND ! I was pooped. and out of TIME. The key factor in all of this. I just wanted to turn something in. so I completed the page and turned everything in.

Yes, I turned ‘er in. And waited.

Fast forward a couple of hours, it turns out my submission won first place in its section, and I got to say a few words, which was nice. üéâ

But, I’m here to tell you what I have learned at this point (Though victory was super sweet especially after all the stress I endured (üòå), and I was proud/impressed with the quick turnaround time I had ! )

  1. Always, always, always have a backup plan.

  2. If you feel like something is not working the first 50 times you try to implement it, especially if you are in a competition setting, please, just move on üò≠ ( You can even come back to it if it’s really that pressing )

  3. Don’t forget to nourish yourself. Yes, I know as Developers, sleepless nights and caffeine-fueled work-sprints are basically “written into our fate”, but… it actually doesn’t have to be. Please. keep a bottle of water near you and just try to get some decent sleep.

    • this prevents you from delirium and making dumb mistakes you otherwise would not have made had you been well-rested and well-fed.
    • and believe it or not, as a result, you’ll be more productive than if you had just skipped out on all those important things.

Thanks for reading ! If you want to check out Prejudmania, then check out my portfolio page.
